The peanut butter and jelly sandwich is a staple in American households. A sandwich consisting of two peices of sliced bread, peanut butter and jelly first appeared in 1901 in the Boston Cooking School Magazine of Culinary Science and Domestic Economics written by Julia Davis Chandler. She said to use currant or crab-apple jelly and called the combination delicious and as far as she knew, original.

Peanut butter and sandwiches have become more popular as speciality restaurants like PB&J Deli which features their signature sandwhich the St. Pete Savory. This sandwich is a mix a savory cashew butter with fig jelly, with sprouts, avocado, and Frank's Red Hot sauce.
